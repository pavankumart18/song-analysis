01. The Challenge

A Labyrinth of Sound

Indian music differs fundamentally from the structured verses of Western pop. It is a continuous tapestry of melody—the Raga—weaving seamlessly between vocalist and instrument. The line where a singer stops and a violin begins is often blurry, even to the human ear.

For an AI accustomed to 4/4 drum beats, this is a nightmare.

We launched an ambitious experiment: Could we build an automated pipeline to listen to a Telugu song and break it down into its cultural soul—Pallavi, Charanam, and Interludes?

03. The Contenders

Demucs vs. SAM

Two AI models entered the arena. Only one survived the noise.

Demucs

The "Audio Purist". Operates on raw waveforms. Reliable, steady, but sometimes conservative with background bleed.

SAM-Audio

The "New Contender". Recently released audio-specific adaptation of Segment Anything. Aggressive noise reduction, but untested on Indian Ragas.

04. The Failure Mode

The "Violin" Problem

In 'Naa Autograph', the AI mistook the violin for a human voice, continuously merging the Chorus and Verse.

Because Indian violin styles often mimic the sliding pitch of the human voice (Gamakam), the AI effectively got "tricked". SAM, trained on images, often saw the visual line of a violin frequency and confidently labeled it VOCAL.
